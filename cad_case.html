Case Study 1

Aircraft space allocation modelling and configuration control

Aircraft are extremely complex products and their design in integration requires robust control in order for the parts to fit within required tolerances as well as cut down on one-off mods required during the assembly process.

The task was to set-up a document and signatory procedure for a small electric aircraft DMU (Digital Mock-Up) in the software Catia. This involved setting up major assembly axis systems and datum points for major sections of the aircraft, interfaces between parts and set up a configuration change control process in place in order to ensure the aircraft design configuration is always in check.
